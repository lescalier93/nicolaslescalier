Bridge Loans Explained: Short‑Term Funding Solutions

A bridge loan is a short‑term financing option used to “bridge” a gap between the purchase of a new property or business opportunity and the receipt of longer‑term financing or sale proceeds.

When to use a bridge loan

  • Buying before selling: Access equity to buy a new property before your current one is sold.
  • Renovation or value‑add projects: Fund renovations, tenant improvements or repositioning until the property can qualify for permanent financing.
  • Time‑sensitive opportunities: Quickly secure cash for auctions or to prevent a deal from falling through while paperwork is finalized.

Typical terms and structure

Bridge loans are usually interest‑only with terms from 6 to 24 months. Rates are higher than long‑term mortgages but lower than hard money loans. Repayment comes from the sale of the existing property or refinance into permanent debt.

Collateral and eligibility

Because bridge loans are secured by real estate or other collateral, lenders look at:

  • Loan‑to‑value (LTV) ratio – often 50‑70% of current or future appraised value
  • Exit strategy – how you plan to repay (sale, refinance, incoming cash)
  • Creditworthiness and experience

Pros and cons

  • Pros: Fast approval, flexible underwriting, and allows you to act quickly.
  • Cons: Higher rates and fees than long‑term loans, and limited term length.
