Term Loans vs Merchant Cash Advances

When your business needs capital, understanding your financing options is crucial. Two common products are term loans and merchant cash advances (MCAs). This article explains the differences and helps you decide which fits your situation.

Repayment Structure

Term loans involve fixed monthly payments over a set period (e.g., 12–60 months). Each payment includes principal and interest. This predictability is ideal for long-term investments and budgeting.

Merchant cash advances are repaid via a percentage of your daily or weekly sales until the agreed payback amount (advance × factor rate) is met. Payments fluctuate with your revenue.

Cost

Term loans typically have lower annualized interest rates, but approval may take longer and require stronger credit. MCAs have higher effective rates due to factor pricing but provide quicker access to funds.

Eligibility

Term loans usually require solid credit scores, time in business, and financial documentation. MCAs are based primarily on consistent card or bank deposits, making them accessible to businesses with shorter histories or lower credit.

Best Use Cases

  • Term loans: equipment purchases, expansions, working capital with predictable returns.
  • MCAs: short-term cash needs, managing cash flow gaps, or seizing timely opportunities.
