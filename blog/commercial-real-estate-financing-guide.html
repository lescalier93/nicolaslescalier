Commercial Real Estate Financing Guide

Commercial real estate (CRE) financing can help business owners acquire, expand or refinance properties used for offices, retail, industrial or mixed‑use. Unlike residential mortgages, CRE loans are tailored to the needs of businesses and investors.

Types of CRE financing

  • Term loans: Fixed rates and monthly payments over 5–25 years, often used for owner‑occupied buildings.
  • Bridge loans: Short‑term financing (6‑24 months) to acquire or renovate properties before permanent financing.
  • Construction loans: Funds released in stages to build or redevelop properties; converts to a term loan once completed.
  • CMBS & agency loans: Securitized loans for stabilized properties with longer amortization and competitive rates.

Eligibility and documentation

Lenders evaluate property cash flow, borrower experience, credit, and down payment. Typical documents include:

  • Two years of financial statements and tax returns
  • Rent roll and operating statements (for investment properties)
  • Appraisal and environmental reports
  • Business plan for value‑add projects

Benefits of CRE financing

Owning commercial property can build equity, stabilize occupancy costs and provide tax benefits. By working with a broker who compares multiple lenders, you can find competitive rates and structures aligned with your strategy.
