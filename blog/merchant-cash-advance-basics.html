Understanding Merchant Cash Advances

Merchant Cash Advances (MCAs) provide quick access to working capital by advancing funds against your business’s future sales. They’re ideal for businesses with steady debit and credit card revenue that need capital fast to seize opportunities.

How MCAs Work

You receive a lump sum of cash up front. In return, a percentage of your daily or weekly sales is remitted until the advance plus fees (expressed as a factor rate) is repaid. MCAs don’t have fixed terms or interest rates; instead, the total payback depends on the factor rate and your remittance schedule.

Benefits of MCAs

  • Speed: Approvals in 24–72 hours.
  • Flexibility: Payments adjust with your revenue; slower sales mean smaller remittances.
  • Credit-friendly: Approval is based more on cash flow than credit score.

Considerations

  • Factor rates typically range from 1.15 to 1.50, which can translate to higher effective costs than term loans.
  • Since remittances are frequent, ensure your cash flow can support them.
