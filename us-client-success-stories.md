  <h1>Examples of financing structures matched to real business constraints.</h1>
  <p class="section-intro">These examples show the type of scenarios I help clients navigate. The common pattern is not just access to capital. It is matching the structure to timing, repayment capacity, and the practical needs of the business.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="grid grid--2">
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">Restaurant Expansion</span>
      <h3>$150,000 working capital loan</h3>
      <p><strong>Situation:</strong> A Texas restaurant group needed fast capital to secure a fourth location and start buildout before losing the site.</p>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong> Working capital structure with a 1.25 factor and a 9-month term, tied to card-based revenue patterns.</p>
      <p><strong>Outcome:</strong> The location opened within six weeks and annual revenue increased materially after launch.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">Manufacturing Upgrade</span>
      <h3>$500,000 term loan</h3>
      <p><strong>Situation:</strong> A Michigan fabricator needed a new CNC machine without accepting a blanket lien across all assets.</p>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong> Fixed-rate term loan secured primarily by the equipment, with early payoff flexibility.</p>
      <p><strong>Outcome:</strong> Production time dropped, new contracts were won, and the company repaid the facility ahead of schedule.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">Contractor Cash Gap</span>
      <h3>$750,000 bridge loan</h3>
      <p><strong>Situation:</strong> A California contractor had a municipal project but needed liquidity before net-60 invoice payments came in.</p>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong> Six-month bridge loan with interest-only payments to cover materials and subcontractor costs.</p>
      <p><strong>Outcome:</strong> The project stayed on schedule, the borrower repaid after receivables landed, and the company won follow-on work.</p>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">Chicago Property</span>
      <h3>CRE refinance with cash-out</h3>
      <p><strong>Situation:</strong> A mixed-use property owner needed to replace an expensive loan and free up capital for improvements.</p>
      <p><strong>Structure:</strong> Long-term refinance at a lower fixed rate with amortization support and a cash-out component.</p>
      <p><strong>Outcome:</strong> Monthly payments fell, renovation capital was unlocked, and property performance improved.</p>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
