# Term Loans for U.S. Businesses — Nicolas Lescalier’s Approach

Term loans are among the most common types of business financing. They provide a lump sum of capital that is repaid over a fixed period with scheduled payments. Rates can be fixed or variable, and terms range from six months to ten years or more depending on the lender and collateral.

## Why term loans?

Term loans are ideal when you need a substantial amount of capital for:

- Buying equipment or vehicles
- Renovating or expanding your premises
- Consolidating high-cost debt
- Acquiring another business
- Investing in long-term projects

They typically offer lower annualized rates than merchant cash advances or short-term products. Fixed payments make budgeting easier, and the cost of capital is often tax-deductible.

## Nicolas’s methodology

Nicolas takes time to understand your business model, revenue patterns and growth plans. For U.S. clients, his approach includes:

1. **Cash flow analysis** — ensuring the monthly payment fits comfortably within your budget.
2. **Term selection** — choosing a term that matches the useful life of the asset being financed.
3. **Collateral assessment** — evaluating whether the loan should be secured or unsecured. Real estate, equipment or invoices can often be pledged to reduce rates.
4. **Bank and non-bank offers** — comparing bank loans, SBA loans and alternative term lenders. He looks beyond the headline rate to total cost, fees and covenants.
5. **Amortization options** — exploring interest-only periods or step-up structures to align with ramp-up revenue.

By working with multiple institutions, Nicolas is able to secure competitive rates and flexible structures tailored to industries like construction, healthcare, retail and professional services.

## Comparing term loans to MCAs

Term loans are slower to fund than MCAs, but they offer longer repayment periods and lower total cost. MCAs are best for emergencies or short-term needs; term loans are suited to significant investments that generate returns over time. ## SBA and bank loans

For creditworthy businesses, Small Business Administration (SBA) loans and bank loans provide some of the most affordable financing. However, they require extensive documentation and collateral. Nicolas assists with compiling the application package and navigating underwriting, helping clients avoid common pitfalls.

For more details, refer to the SBA’s [Loan Programs](https://www.sba.gov/funding-programs/loans).
