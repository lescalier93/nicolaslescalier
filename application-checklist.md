<section class="section">
  <p class="eyebrow">Application Checklist</p>
  <h1>What to prepare before you apply for business financing.</h1>
  <p class="section-intro">The cleaner the file, the better the conversation. You do not need every document for every product, but you should have the basic deal facts ready before expecting a useful answer.</p>
</section>

<section class="section">
  <div class="grid grid--2">
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">1. Deal Basics</span>
      <ul class="plain-list">
        <li>Requested amount or target range</li>
        <li>Use of proceeds</li>
        <li>Preferred funding timeline</li>
        <li>Product you think you need, if any</li>
      </ul>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">2. Business Profile</span>
      <ul class="plain-list">
        <li>Industry and state</li>
        <li>Time in business</li>
        <li>Estimated monthly or annual revenue</li>
        <li>Whether the business is seasonal, project-based, or recurring-revenue driven</li>
      </ul>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">3. Financial Support</span>
      <ul class="plain-list">
        <li>Recent bank statements</li>
        <li>Tax returns, if available</li>
        <li>P&amp;L or operating statements, if available</li>
        <li>Existing debt or repayment obligations</li>
      </ul>
    </article>
    <article class="service-card">
      <span class="card-label">4. Product-Specific Items</span>
      <ul class="plain-list">
        <li>Invoices for factoring</li>
        <li>Purchase orders and supplier path for PO finance</li>
        <li>Equipment quote for equipment financing</li>
        <li>Property details, value, and current debt terms for CRE or mortgage scenarios</li>
      </ul>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="section split">
  <div class="panel">
    <p class="eyebrow">What slows a file down</p>
    <ul class="plain-list">
      <li>Unclear use of proceeds</li>
      <li>Borrower asks for a product that does not fit the scenario</li>
      <li>Missing revenue or bank-statement visibility</li>
      <li>Undisclosed debt, liens, or collateral constraints</li>
      <li>Time pressure without the supporting facts ready</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
  <div class="proof-card">
    <p class="eyebrow">What helps</p>
    <p>A short, organized summary of the business and the need is often more valuable at the start than sending too many random documents without context.</p>
  </div>
</section>
